My patient identifies as Two-Spirited. What does that mean?
EduCareAI · Clinical Guideline
EduCareAI · Clinical Guideline
Two-Spirit (2S) is a term used by some Indigenous people to describe their sexual, gender, and spiritual identity. It is important to recognize that it is not merely an "Indigenous version" of being LGBTQ+ — it is a specific cultural role that was largely suppressed by colonial systems and is now being reclaimed.
Origin of the Term

The term was proposed in 1990 at the third annual Intertribal Native American/First Nations Gay and Lesbian American Conference in Winnipeg. It serves as an umbrella term for Indigenous people who carry both a masculine and feminine spirit.

  • A Spiritual Identity: For many, 2S describes a sacred, traditional role within the community — such as being a healer, a name-giver, or a mediator.
  • Distinct from Western Labels: While a 2S person may also identify as gay, trans, or non-binary, "Two-Spirit" emphasizes the intersection of their Indigenous identity and gender/sexuality.
  • Nation-Specific: Many Nations have their own terms that predate "Two-Spirit" (e.g., Winkté in Lakota, Nàdleehé in Navajo, or A'nó:wà:ne in some Haudenosaunee contexts).

EduCareAI · Clinical Guideline
The goal is to move from cultural competence (knowing facts) to cultural safety — where the patient feels genuinely respected and empowered throughout their care.
1 · Impact of Colonialism

Mental health challenges in 2S individuals are often rooted in intergenerational trauma. Residential schools targeted gender-diverse roles for erasure, replacing them with strict Western binaries.

Consideration: Recognize that reclaiming their 2S identity may be a core part of your patient's healing journey from colonial trauma.
2 · Holistic Framework

Wellness for a 2S patient often requires balance across all four quadrants of the Medicine Wheel: Physical, Emotional, Mental, and Spiritual.

Consideration: Ask if the patient has access to traditional healing, Elders, or Knowledge Keepers. In Toronto, Anishnawbe Health offers integrated care combining clinical therapy with traditional ceremonies.
3 · Affirming Communication
  • Ask about pronouns and terms: "Two-Spirit" is an umbrella — your patient may prefer a Nation-specific term.
  • Assess social belonging: Some 2S individuals face "double discrimination" — within their own community and from the Western LGBTQ+ community. Isolation is a high-risk factor for suicide and substance use.
  • Avoid pathologizing: Ensure their identity is seen as a source of strength and resilience, not a disorder or symptom.
